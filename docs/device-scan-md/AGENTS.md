# Engineering Content Policy

Version: 1.0

Status: Governing Policy

Applies to:

- DeviceScan
- Engineering Library
- Manufacturer Portal
- Subscriber Library
- AI Services
- Publisher Portal
- Librarian Portal
- Public Library

---

# Purpose

This document defines the governing principles for all engineering information managed by LineWatt.

These principles take precedence over implementation convenience.

Every feature, workflow, API, AI agent and user interface must comply with this policy.

---

# Platform Intent

LineWatt is an engineering intelligence platform.

It is **not** a public repository of copyrighted manufacturer documentation.

Its primary purpose is to build and maintain a trusted Canonical Engineering Library for renewable energy equipment.

---

# Canonical Engineering Library

The primary product of LineWatt is the canonical engineering database.

The library contains:

- normalized engineering parameters
- engineering metadata
- compatibility information
- engineering calculations
- review history
- provenance
- engineering notes
- confidence scoring

This database is the primary intellectual property of LineWatt.

---

# Public Manufacturer Information

LineWatt may index publicly available manufacturer engineering information for the purpose of creating canonical engineering data.

For manufacturers that have not granted hosting permission:

- original documents shall not be publicly redistributed
- users should be directed to the manufacturer's official download pages
- engineering summaries may be presented
- provenance must be retained

---

# Subscriber Documents

Subscribers may upload their own documents for private engineering analysis.

Examples include:

- datasheets
- manuals
- certificates
- reports

Subscriber documents remain private unless the subscriber explicitly contributes them.

---

# Featured Manufacturers

Featured Manufacturers may authorize LineWatt to:

- host official datasheets
- distribute official documents
- publish manuals
- publish certificates
- publish firmware
- maintain revision history
- answer subscriber technical questions
- publish official engineering notices

Official Manufacturer Content shall be clearly identified.

---

# Engineering Summaries

Engineering summaries are created by LineWatt.

They are not official manufacturer documentation unless explicitly designated otherwise.

Engineering summaries are provided:

- as is
- as available

They may contain:

- OCR errors
- AI errors
- software defects
- transcription errors
- interpretation errors
- manual review errors

Users are responsible for independently verifying engineering information before relying upon it.

---

# Warranty

Except for Official Manufacturer Content published by Featured Manufacturers,

LineWatt engineering artefacts shall not be represented as:

- official manufacturer documentation
- warranty documentation
- contractual documentation
- certification evidence
- legally authoritative specifications

Manufacturer documentation remains the authoritative source for warranty, procurement, installation and certification.

---

# Content Provenance

Engineering artefacts shall retain provenance wherever practical.

Examples include:

- source
- revision
- retrieval date
- verification status
- reviewer
- confidence

---

# Copyright

Manufacturer copyrights remain with the respective copyright holders.

Manufacturer trademarks remain the property of their respective owners.

LineWatt claims no ownership over original manufacturer documentation.

---

# AI Generated Content

AI generated engineering artefacts are engineering assistance.

They are not engineering certification.

They are not professional engineering approval.

---

# Product Principle

The value of LineWatt is not document storage.

The value of LineWatt is trusted engineering knowledge.

---

# Non-Negotiable Principles

1. LineWatt is an engineering intelligence platform.

2. The Canonical Engineering Library is the primary product.

3. Public manufacturer documents shall not be redistributed without authorization.

4. Subscriber documents remain private.

5. Featured Manufacturers may authorize official hosting.

6. Engineering provenance shall be preserved.

7. Engineering summaries are not official manufacturer documentation.

8. Official manufacturer documentation remains authoritative.

9. Every implementation must comply with this policy.

10. If an implementation conflicts with this policy, development shall stop until an explicit policy decision is made.

11. LineWatt seeks to strengthen the relationship between engineers and manufacturers. Platform features should encourage manufacturer participation, attribution, and engagement rather than replace or compete with the manufacturer's official engineering resources.
