## 1. Purpose and scope

This guide controls every document in this framework. Draft for engineers, manufacturers, subscribers, procurement teams, regulators, and counsel in concise professional English.

## 2. Normative language

- **must/must not**: obligation or prohibition;
- **may**: permission or discretion;
- **should**: recommendation, not an obligation;
- **will**: stated future action, used only when operationally supportable.

Avoid archaic legalese, marketing claims, absolute security or compliance claims, and unsupported promises.

## 3. Defined terms

3.1 Use capitalisation only for terms in [Legal Definitions](./LEGAL_DEFINITIONS.md), ordinary proper nouns, document titles, status labels, and sentence starts.

3.2 Do not redefine shared terms. Add a shared definition only when it will recur. Use “Registered User” and “Subscriber” distinctly; use the product label “My Private Datasets.”

## 4. Structure and numbering

4.1 Use one H1. Use sequential H2 headings and H3 only beneath an H2. Number substantive clauses `1`, `1.1`, and `1.1.1` when useful for reference.

4.2 Every public agreement, policy, or notice uses the prescribed metadata block, prominent draft warning, Purpose, Scope, Applicable definitions, tailored substantive clauses, Related documents, contact/complaints route when appropriate, and Revision history.

4.2.1 A Draft uses `**Proposed Effective Date:** [TO BE CONFIRMED]`. It must not use an operative `Effective Date`. The `Effective Date` field is used only after a document is Approved for publication or execution.

4.3 Use bullets for non-sequential examples, numbered lists for ordered steps, and tables only for genuine mappings. Keep tables narrow and complete.

## 5. Links and cross-references

Use repository-relative Markdown links and the official document name. Link rather than repeat. Refer to a clause only if stable; otherwise link to the named document. No absolute local paths or unverified external citations.

## 6. Rights drafting

Distinguish ownership, licence, access, hosting, processing, display, distribution, confidentiality, database rights, copyright, trademarks, and contract restrictions. Do not claim ownership of third-party Content or unprotectable facts. Describe Compiled Datasheets and exports as potentially mixed-rights outputs.

## 7. Engineering and AI drafting

State capabilities conditionally and match repository evidence. Automated, rules-based, AI-assisted, and human review may each be involved. Never imply certification, guaranteed accuracy, autonomous professional judgment, or a verified training/data-use practice without evidence.

## 8. Privacy, security, and commercial drafting

8.1 Privacy terminology must be consistent with applicable Data Protection Law and GDPR. Controller, Processor, Personal Data, Processing, Data Subject, and Personal Data Breach retain their statutory meanings unless expressly stated otherwise. Identify Controller and Processor roles by context; do not guarantee “GDPR compliance.” Use verified data maps, vendor terms, and transfer mechanisms.

8.2 Describe security at a control-family level without exploitable detail. Do not use “secure,” “encrypted,” “anonymous,” or “never” as absolutes unless evidenced and scoped.

8.3 Do not hard-code prices, transient limits, Paddle capacity, refund windows, uptime, credits, support targets, liability caps, or retention periods unless approved.

## 9. Placeholders and uncertainty

Use conspicuous square-bracket placeholders such as `[LINEWATT LEGAL ENTITY]` and `[TO BE CONFIRMED]`. Never use plausible invented facts. Before publication, each placeholder must be resolved, expressly retained as a public variable, or recorded as non-applicable.

## 10. Dates, versions, and citations

Use ISO dates (`YYYY-MM-DD`) in registers and unambiguous written dates in public text. Versions follow [Legal Versioning](./LEGAL_VERSIONING.md). Cite current law only when necessary, by jurisdiction and authoritative source; counsel must verify citations before approval.

## 11. Appendices and change control

Appendices must identify whether operational or contractual. A contractual annex follows the agreement it supplements. Every change updates revision history, the framework changelog, affected matrices, links, and acceptance analysis.
