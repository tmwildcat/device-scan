## 1. Purpose, scope, and precedence

This DPA applies where LineWatt processes Personal Data as Processor for an Enterprise Customer as Controller under an Enterprise Agreement and Order. It prevails only concerning conflicts about the processing or protection of Personal Data. Where GDPR applies, this DPA is intended to address the processor-contract requirements in Article 28 GDPR.

## 2. Applicable definitions

Capitalised terms use [Legal Definitions](../LEGAL_DEFINITIONS.md); applicable data-protection law controls inconsistent statutory terminology.

## 3. Processing details

Subject matter: provision of ordered Platform, workspace, support, API, or MCP Services. Duration: the Order plus approved return, deletion, backup, and legal-hold periods. Nature and purpose: hosting, access control, compilation, search, support, security, export, and other documented Order functions. Data Subjects, categories, and special-category restrictions are specified in Annex 1 and must be completed per Order.

## 4. Processor duties

LineWatt must process only documented lawful instructions; notify the Customer if an instruction appears unlawful; ensure authorised personnel confidentiality; implement Annex 2 measures; keep required records; and not sell or independently use Customer-controlled Personal Data except as legally permitted and disclosed. The parties must assess and implement security appropriate to risk where Article 32 GDPR or corresponding Data Protection Law applies.

## 5. Subprocessors

Customer gives `[GENERAL OR SPECIFIC AUTHORISATION]`. LineWatt will provide `[NOTICE PERIOD]` notice of additions and a reasonable, legally reviewed objection process. Subprocessors must receive materially equivalent data-protection duties. Current entries belong in the [Subprocessor Register](../appendices/SUBPROCESSORS.md).

## 6. Assistance and incidents

Taking account of Processing and available information, LineWatt will reasonably assist with Data Subject requests, security, Personal Data Breach notification, data-protection impact assessments, and regulator consultation. LineWatt will notify Customer of a relevant Personal Data Breach without undue delay at `[SECURITY CONTACT EMAIL]`, supporting the Customer's obligations under Article 33 GDPR where applicable; notice is not an admission of fault. Assistance with impact assessments and prior consultation must address Article 35 GDPR and related requirements where applicable.

## 7. Return, deletion, and audits

At termination and Customer choice, LineWatt will return or delete Customer Personal Data unless law requires retention, subject to documented backup cycles. Customer may receive compliance information and conduct a proportionate audit `[AUDIT TERMS TO BE CONFIRMED]`, protecting other customers, confidentiality, and security.

## 8. International transfers
