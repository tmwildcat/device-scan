## 1. Purpose and scope

## 2. Applicable definitions

## 3. Payment provider and seller

LineWatt expects to use Paddle as a Payment Provider. At checkout, the seller and any merchant-of-record role must be identified as `[SELLER / MERCHANT OF RECORD TO BE CONFIRMED]`. The checkout disclosure controls transaction mechanics but does not remove mandatory rights.

## 4. Fees, taxes, and renewal

Current Fees, currency, billing cycle, tax treatment, start date, renewal, and included entitlements appear at checkout or in an Order. Unless checkout states otherwise and law permits, a recurring Subscription renews for the disclosed period until cancelled before `[CANCELLATION DEADLINE]`.

## 5. Cancellation and plan changes

Cancellation stops future renewal but ordinarily does not end access before the paid period expires. Upgrade, downgrade, proration, and effective timing are `[TO BE CONFIRMED]` and must be disclosed before confirmation.

## 6. Refunds and statutory rights

6.1 The voluntary refund window and eligibility are `[REFUND RULES TO BE CONFIRMED]`. Duplicate or erroneous charges will be investigated and corrected where verified.

6.2 Statutory cooling-off, cancellation, refund, and consumer remedies apply notwithstanding this Policy. Any request to begin digital service during a cooling-off period and any waiver must use a legally valid flow.

## 7. Failed payments, chargebacks, and disputes

LineWatt or the Payment Provider may retry payment and restrict paid access after notice. Users should contact `[BILLING CONTACT EMAIL]` before an avoidable chargeback. A good-faith billing dispute does not waive legal rights; fraudulent chargebacks or payment abuse may trigger investigation.

## 8. Enterprise Orders and contact

Enterprise invoicing, taxes, purchase orders, and refunds follow the Order. Complaints may be sent to `[BILLING CONTACT EMAIL]` and escalated through `[PAYMENT PROVIDER DISPUTE ROUTE]`.
