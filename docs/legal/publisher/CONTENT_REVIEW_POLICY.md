## 1. Purpose and scope

This Policy governs review, validation, lifecycle, publication, correction, and provenance for Engineering Records.

## 2. Applicable definitions

Capitalised terms use [Legal Definitions](../LEGAL_DEFINITIONS.md).

## 3. Review dimensions

3.1 The Platform may separately record an Engineering Record's lifecycle, Review Status, and Validation Status so that publication state, human review, and validation results are not conflated.

3.2 Product documentation and interfaces may identify the available workflow stages. Those implementation values are not contractual definitions and may evolve without changing the legal meaning of Review Status or Validation Status.

## 4. Workflow and permissions

Private records remain private and may be managed only within authorised tenant workflows. Manufacturer or Publisher submissions cannot publish directly. Authorised LineWatt staff review central records; validation errors require the approved override role and recorded reason. A warning may remain visible when a record is published only under approved rules.

## 5. Review criteria and effect

Review may assess authority, source, revision, extraction, units, schema, completeness, internal consistency, provenance, and validation warnings. Review and approval reduce identified risk but do not guarantee authenticity, accuracy, completeness, suitability, regulatory approval, or currentness.

## 6. Corrections and history

LineWatt may flag, reject, correct, discontinue, replace, unpublish, or preserve historical records. Replacements should link provenance where supported. Safety and recall issues receive priority under `[SAFETY ESCALATION PROCESS]`. Audit data must avoid unnecessary sensitive payloads.

## 7. Appeals and contact

Submit evidence to `[ENGINEERING CORRECTIONS EMAIL]`. Affected Publishers may appeal through `[PUBLISHER APPEAL ROUTE]`; LineWatt may impose interim restriction while reviewing risk.
