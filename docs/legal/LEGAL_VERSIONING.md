## 1. Purpose and scope

This standard governs identification, status, approval, publication, notification, acceptance, and retention for every legal-framework document.

## 2. Versions and statuses

2.1 Use `MAJOR.MINOR`. A major version changes legal effect, allocation of risk, purposes of processing, or acceptance scope. A minor version makes compatible clarification or non-material correction.

2.2 Permitted statuses are: **Draft** (under development), **Approved** (authorised but not necessarily live), **Published** (operative at an approved location), **Superseded** (replaced but retained), and **Archived** (retained internally and not operative).

2.3 Draft metadata uses `1.0 Draft` and `**Proposed Effective Date:** [TO BE CONFIRMED]`. A Draft must not use an operative `Effective Date`. The `Effective Date` field is used only after a document is Approved for publication or execution. Approval records must identify approvers and decision date.

## 3. Dates and release record

Each Published document records publication date, effective date, version, stable URL, approvers, affected audience, notice method, and acceptance requirement. Effective dates must allow any mandatory notice period.

## 4. Change classification

4.1 Material changes include new fees or renewal rules, new processing purposes, broader content licences, reduced privacy or access rights, new dispute terms, material liability changes, or materially changed API/data restrictions.

4.2 Non-material changes include typographical corrections, clearer wording without changed effect, updated contact details, and non-substantive formatting.

4.3 Counsel decides disputed classifications.

## 5. Notice and re-acceptance

Material changes require notice proportionate to law, contract, and impact and affirmative re-acceptance where required. Product owners must prevent continued gated use when re-acceptance is required. Non-material changes may use an in-product notice or changelog unless law requires more.

## 6. Retention and evidence

Retain exact Published text, incorporated policies, acceptance evidence, notices, approval records, and change rationale for `[LEGAL RECORD RETENTION PERIOD]`, subject to legal hold and applicable law. Never overwrite the only operative copy.
