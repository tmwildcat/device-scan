## 1. Purpose and scope

This Policy provides a channel for good-faith vulnerability reports concerning `[IN-SCOPE DOMAINS AND SERVICES]`.

## 2. Applicable definitions

Capitalised terms use [Legal Definitions](../LEGAL_DEFINITIONS.md).

## 3. Authorised conduct

Researchers must use their own Accounts and data; minimise access and impact; stop when encountering Personal Data, Private Datasets, credentials, or destructive capability; preserve confidentiality; avoid persistence; and report promptly. Prohibited activity includes denial of service, social engineering, physical attacks, malware, extortion, automated high-volume scanning without approval, data alteration/deletion, tenant crossover exploitation beyond minimal proof, and privacy invasion.

## 4. Reporting

Send `[SECURITY CONTACT EMAIL]` a clear description, affected asset, prerequisites, reproducible steps, minimal evidence, impact, and safe remediation suggestion. Encrypt reports using `[SECURITY PGP KEY/LINK]` if available. Do not include unnecessary sensitive data.

## 5. Response and safe-harbour intent

LineWatt intends to acknowledge within `[TARGET]`, triage, communicate, remediate based on risk, and coordinate disclosure. Subject to applicable law and compliance with this Policy, LineWatt intends not to pursue claims solely for the good-faith authorised research; it cannot bind third parties or authorities. Counsel must approve this language.

## 6. Confidentiality and rewards

Researchers must not disclose before written coordination or `[DISCLOSURE PERIOD]`. No reward or bug bounty is promised. LineWatt may acknowledge a researcher only with consent.
