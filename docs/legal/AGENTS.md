**Version:** 1.0
**Project:** LineWatt Library Legal Framework
**Status:** Active

---

## Purpose

This repository contains the official legal framework for the LineWatt platform.

The objective is **not** to generate generic SaaS legal templates.

The objective is to create a professional, internally consistent, maintainable legal framework specifically designed for the LineWatt ecosystem, including engineering data extraction, manufacturer participation, AI-assisted processing, subscriber services, enterprise deployments, and future platform expansion.

The legal framework is considered a core component of the LineWatt platform architecture.

---

## Design Philosophy

The legal framework should be developed with the same engineering discipline applied to the software platform.

Documents should be:

* modular
* maintainable
* version controlled
* internally consistent
* cross-referenced
* written in clear professional English
* suitable for legal review

Avoid duplicated concepts.

Avoid contradictory clauses.

Avoid unnecessary repetition.

---

## Read Order

Before drafting or modifying any legal document, contributors shall read the following documents in order:

1. `LEGAL_ARCHITECTURE.md`
2. `LEGAL_DEFINITIONS.md`
3. `LEGAL_STYLE_GUIDE.md`
4. `LEGAL_VERSIONING.md`

## Primary Objectives

Every legal document should support one or more of the following objectives:

* Define contractual relationships.
* Protect intellectual property.
* Explain engineering data processing.
* Protect user privacy.
* Support regulatory compliance.
* Support compliance with applicable privacy and data protection laws, including the UK GDPR, EU GDPR, and related legislation where applicable.
* Enable enterprise adoption.
* Clearly allocate responsibilities between LineWatt and platform users.

## Guiding Principles

## Clarity

Write for engineers, manufacturers, EPC contractors, consultants, enterprise customers, and software developers.

Use modern professional English.

Avoid unnecessary legal jargon.

---

## Consistency

Every defined term shall have exactly one meaning.

Definitions belong exclusively in `LEGAL_DEFINITIONS.md`.

Never redefine a term elsewhere.

---

## Modularity

Every document should address a single legal subject.

Avoid copying text between documents.

Cross-reference instead.

---

## Maintainability

Assume the framework will evolve over many years.

Future contributors should be able to update one document without breaking the remainder of the framework.

---

## Originality

All content must be original.

Do not copy legal templates.

Do not reproduce third-party terms.

Do not adapt copyrighted material.

Reference legal concepts where appropriate but write original documentation.

## Intellectual Property Principles

The framework must distinguish between:

* Manufacturer Content
* User Content
* Private Datasets
* Engineering Records
* Canonical Datasets
* Engineering Metadata
* Search Indexes
* AI-generated derivative information
* Platform Software
* Platform Documentation

Ownership, licensing, and permitted use must remain internally consistent across all documents.

---

## Engineering Principles

The legal framework must recognise that:

* engineering information may originate from multiple sources;
* AI assists engineering workflows;
* engineering outputs may require human review;
* users remain responsible for engineering decisions and regulatory compliance.

Engineering assistance is not engineering certification.

---

## Privacy Principles

The privacy framework shall be designed to support compliance with applicable data protection legislation, including where applicable:

* UK GDPR;
* EU GDPR;
* UK Data Protection Act 2018;
* ePrivacy rules;
* applicable national implementations; and
* future international privacy regulations adopted by LineWatt.

The framework should support lawful processing, transparency, accountability, privacy by design, and data minimisation.

## Writing Standards

Use:

* numbered clauses;
* descriptive headings;
* concise paragraphs;
* defined terminology;
* consistent formatting.

Avoid:

* unnecessary repetition;
* undefined terms;
* ambiguous wording;
* excessive legalese;
* marketing language.

---

## Language Guidelines

Prefer:

* must
* may
* should
* will

Avoid archaic expressions such as:

* hereinafter
* aforesaid
* whereas
* thereto
* hereinbefore
* notwithstanding the foregoing

Use modern legal drafting style.

---

## Document Structure

Unless there is a compelling reason otherwise, documents should contain:

1. Purpose
2. Scope
3. Definitions (by reference)
4. Rights
5. Responsibilities
6. Restrictions
7. Applicable Policies
8. Revision History

---

## Cross Referencing

Documents should reference:

* Terms of Use
* Privacy Policy
* Engineering Disclaimer
* Copyright Policy
* Subscriber Agreement
* Manufacturer Agreement

where appropriate.

Avoid repeating entire clauses.

---

## Quality Standards

A document is not complete unless it:

* uses only defined terminology;
* introduces no contradictory obligations;
* contains no duplicated legal concepts;
* references applicable policies;
* maintains consistent language with the remainder of the framework;
* accurately reflects the LineWatt platform.

---

## Review Checklist

Before committing changes, verify that:

* all defined terms are capitalised correctly;
* every reference resolves to an existing document;
* section numbering is sequential;
* revision history is updated;
* document version is correct;
* no placeholder text remains.

## AI Contributor Guidance

## Operational Legal Governance Roles

The governed platform lifecycle uses only these legal operations roles:

* Legal Publisher may author Drafts, create versions, submit them for review, and view review and publication status. Legal Publisher must not approve, schedule, publish, withdraw, or archive.
* Legal Counsel may author, review, approve, schedule, publish, withdraw, and archive. Legal Counsel may approve their own authored document, but submission, approval, and publication remain separate recorded actions.
* Super Administrator receives the same operational authority through the platform-level override.

Approved content and governed metadata must not be edited in place. A correction requires Return to Draft, editing, resubmission, and fresh approval.

AI contributors should think like documentation architects rather than template generators.

When drafting:

* understand the platform first;
* identify the legal objective;
* reuse established terminology;
* minimise duplication;
* preserve consistency across the framework;
* anticipate future expansion.

If uncertain, improve the architecture before adding more content.

---

## Version Control

Every document shall include:

* Document Name
* Version
* Status
* Effective Date
* Revision History

Major revisions should increment the major version.

Editorial revisions should increment the minor version.

## Long-Term Vision

The LineWatt Legal Framework is intended to become a comprehensive legal reference for the LineWatt ecosystem.

It should evolve alongside the platform while maintaining a stable, coherent legal architecture that supports users, manufacturers, subscribers, enterprise customers, partners, regulators, and future products.

Legal documentation is treated as part of the platform architecture and should be developed with the same level of discipline, review, and version control as the software itself.
